Before the 2022 New York Yankees season, Judge was unsuccessful in negotiating a long-term contract with the Yankees. General manager Brian Cashman told ESPN that the team offered Judge $17 million in arbitration and a seven-year extension worth $213.5 million. Cashman refused to comment on whether this affected the negotiations.

Judge avoided salary arbitration and signed a one-year, $19 million contract on June 24. In 2022, Judge led the major leagues with 62 home runs, 133 runs scored, 131 RBIs, and 111 walks, and batted .311/.425/.686 with 177 hits, 28 doubles, and 19 intentional walks, while stealing 16 bases in 19 attempts.

He played 78 games in center field, 73 games in right field, and 25 games as a designated hitter.

For his play in the 2022 regular season, Judge won the 2022 American League MVP Award, earning 28 of 30 first-place votes.

Judge's 2022 campaign is considered one of the best offensive seasons in Major League history.

The general opinion around the league is that Judge is too good of a player for him to not be able to have another fantastic season, but the number of homeruns he'll hit next season is likely to be significantly less than last year's.

The 2022 season opened without Carlos Correa at shortstop for the first time since 2016 as he left for the Minnesota Twins in free agency. Rookie shortstop Jeremy Peña made the Opening Day start and manned the position for the entire season.

The Astros clinched the division on September 19, their second consecutive division title and their fifth in six seasons. The Astros went on to defeat the Phillies in six games, earning their second World Series title.
Jeremy Peña won the Series MVP Award, the first rookie position player in MLB history to do so, and the first rookie shortstop in history to hit a home run in the World Series.

The Astros are hoping to recreate their magical 2022 season, and in order to do that, several moves were made :

SP Christian Javier agreed to a five-year contract extension.

1B Jose Abreu signed a three-year contract deal.

OF Michael Brantley agreed to a one-year contract.

SP Justin Verlander was lost in free agency to the New York Mets.

Experts graded Houston's offseason a B-, will it be enough to maintain the title?
